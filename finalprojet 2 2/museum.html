Cleveland Museum of Art


CMA was founded in 1913 and opened to the public on June 6, 1916, welcoming 191,547 visitors during its Inaugural Exhibition. The museum was founded by Hinman B. Hurlbut, John Huntington, and Horace Kelley. Jeptha H. Wade II donated land which the museum sits on today. The museum collection houses almost 45,000 objects. The museum offers many programs for children and adults. 1 Wade Oval
Cleveland, Ohio 44106
Phone:216-231-4600
Cleveland Museum of Natural History


The Cleveland Museum of Natural History was founded in 1920 and features 2 distinguished exhibits. "Lucy" an Australopithecus afarensis is a 130 million year old ancestor of man. The second, Haplocanthosaurus delfsi, a 150 million year old sauropod which is one of the most complete skeletons on exhibit in the world. Additional attractions include a planetarium and an observatory. The museum offers many classes and programs for children. There is an onsite wildlife center and features live animal shows. The Museum is involved in research with an active conservation program. Mission Statement: To inspire, through science and education, a passion for nature, the protection of natural diversity, the fostering of health, and leadership to a sustainable future.

Children's Museum of Cleveland


The Children's Museum of Cleveland was established in 1981. Responding to the community need for outreach programs the musuem was founded by the collaboration of parents, educators and civic leaders. The museum offers enrichment through interactive exhibits that reach children visually and sensorially stimulating their imagination. The museum gears its interactive exhibits to children 8 years and younger. Vision and Mission Statements: The Vision: The Children's Museum of Cleveland is to be "the" Northeast Ohio destination and resource for all young children, parents and caregivers to engage in fun, innovative, interactive play that accelerates the child's creativity, developement and education. The Mission: In line with community, market, and societal needs, The Children's Musuem of Cleveland is dedicated to providing unique, innovative, and impactful experiences, exhibits, and education programming, within and outside of the Museum, that will foster learning and development in all children ages birth-8; with a special emphasis on birth-5.

Rock and Roll Hall of Fame


The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame opened in 1995 to celebrate music and musicians who have influenced music and culture around the world. The musuem was established by the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Foundation and Cleveland was chosen after considering other locations including New York, Philadelphia, New Orleans, San Francisco, Memphis and Chicago. I.M. Pei designed the museum on the shores of Lake Erie which has welcomed over eight million visitors. It houses collections of artifacts from John Lennon, The Beatles, Pete Townshend among others and multiple exhibitions throughout the year. The Hall of Fame offers extensive outreach and educational programs to enrich students on the influence of rock and roll and to encourage music appreciation. Mission Statement: The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Museum, Inc. is the nonprofit organization that exist to educate visitors, fans and scholars from around the world about the history and continuing significance of rock and roll music. It carries out this mission throughist operation of a world-class museum that collects, preserves, exhibits and inerprets this art form and through its library and archives as well as its educational programs.

Cleveland Museum of Contemporary Art


MOCA is Cleveland's musuem of contemporary art. It was originally established in 1968 by Marjorie Talalay and Nina Castelli and was originally located in a storefront on Euclid Avenue. The original name of the musuem was called the New Gallery but was officially changed to the Museum of Contemporary Art Cleveland in 2002. It is a non-collecting museum that exhibits new artists around the world but does not maintain a permanent collection. It has been previously located in a frat house as well as the Galleria downtown before it moved to the Cleveland Playhouse where it remained for 22 years. It recently opened in its new permanent location on Mayfield and Euclid Avenue. The new building was designed by Farshid Moussavi of London.
